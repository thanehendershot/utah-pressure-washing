Frequently Asked Questions

What surfaces can you clean?

We can safely clean almost any exterior surface, including:

  • Vinyl, wood, stucco, and brick siding
  • Concrete driveways, sidewalks, and patios
  • Wood and composite decks
  • Fences (wood, vinyl, metal)
  • Commercial storefronts and parking lots
  • Gutters and downspouts

If you're unsure about a specific surface, just ask! We'll assess it during your free quote.

Will pressure washing damage my property?

Not when done correctly! We adjust pressure levels based on the surface material. For delicate surfaces like siding and painted wood, we use soft washing—a low-pressure technique that's gentle yet highly effective. For durable surfaces like concrete, we use higher pressure to deep clean without damage.

Are you licensed and insured?

Yes! We are fully licensed and carry comprehensive liability insurance and workers' compensation coverage. Your property and our team are fully protected.

Is your cleaning solution safe for plants and pets?

Absolutely. We use eco-friendly, biodegradable cleaning solutions that are safe for landscaping, pets, and people. We also take precautions to protect your plants during the cleaning process.

How often should I have my property pressure washed?

We recommend:

  • Driveways and walkways: Once a year
  • House exteriors: Every 1–2 years
  • Decks and fences: Annually, especially before staining or sealing
  • Commercial properties: Quarterly or bi-annually

Regular cleaning prevents buildup, protects surfaces, and maintains curb appeal.

Do I need to be home during the service?

Not necessarily. As long as we have access to an outdoor water source and the areas to be cleaned, we can work while you're away. We'll communicate the schedule and confirm completion.

What's the best time of year for pressure washing in Utah?

Spring through fall (April–October) is ideal. However, we can work year-round during dry spells when temperatures are above freezing.

How do I prepare for my appointment?

Just a few simple steps:

  • Clear the area of furniture, toys, and vehicles
  • Close windows and doors
  • Move potted plants or delicate items
  • Provide access to an outdoor water spigot

We'll handle the rest!
